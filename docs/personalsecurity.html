You Are The Front Line Of Defense

Knowing how to prevent attacks from every source:

In order to prevent everything from major viral infection to the tinniest of spam emails, knowing your own habits is key. You have to start prevention from the source. And that is yourself. We commonly don't read long disclaimers and see ourselves as pioneers of surfing the web. But there is a fatal truth. There will always be someone who is one step ahead, and someone who knows how you think and how to do it better. You're job is simply to make sure you never meet.

Virtual Private Networks

In order to protect some of your connections and to prevent hackers from being able to track you back to your IP address attached to your computer, many use VPNs. VPNs, known as Virtual Private Networks, can aid is masking your computers location, along with IP, previous webpages, and other information vital to tracking you.

Password Protection

One of the easiest ways to stop cyber attacks is to manage your information in password protected application, preferably local, rather than saving your passwords to a database online such as Google or Microsoft Edge. These can easily be detected and through manipulation of source page material, be remotely accessed through your account. A single account should not be used to manage all of your other existing ones.

Reducing Your Carbon Footprint

We leave a big trail whenever we search or click on anything in a webpage. In order to reduce the amount of "carbon footprint," or the trail we leave on any site, there are certain browser functions that can be changed to reduce this trail. Tor, for example, is built in with features for exactly this purpose.
